№ 03 · The manifesto

We're not here to whisper.
We're here to stir things up.

Sakaza exists because the pantry shelf had grown polite, predictable, plastic-wrapped. The bottles got smaller. The flavours got duller. The labels grew louder while the food grew quieter. So we started cooking — properly, in a kitchen in Mumbai — and we haven't stopped since.

01 — Flavour first

Bold is not a bonus. It's the whole point.

Every Sakaza recipe starts with a chef asking a single question — does this make you stop chewing for a second? If the answer is no, it doesn't ship. We don't water down for the supermarket aisle. We don't strip the heat for the mass market.

What lives in the tin is what came out of the wok. What lives in the bottle is what the chef tasted on the spoon. Nothing softer. Nothing safer.

02 — Inspired, not imitated

The cocktail-bar attitude. Zero of the booze.

Wasabi Gin. Spicy Tequila. Fireball Vodka. We borrow the personality of the world's most iconic spirits — the smoke, the heat, the swagger — and bottle it without a single drop of alcohol. The result: a sauce that walks into the dinner like it owns the place.

Safe for kids, gift-able to chefs, equally at home on a sunday brunch table and a tuesday taco night.

03 — Tinned, not plasticked

Packaging that respects the contents.

Sauces in glass. Noodles in tin. No flimsy sachets, no laminated foil, no microplastics waiting to leach into your dinner. Both materials are infinitely recyclable and keep flavour locked in long after the seal breaks.

It costs us more. It makes the product heavier to ship. We took the trade. Some things matter more than logistics.

The philosophy

Innovation is the
secret sauce to a life
not-so-ordinary.

Sakaza was built to stir things up. Our sauces borrow their personality from the world's most iconic spirits — Tequila, Vodka, Gin — without a single drop of alcohol. Our noodles travel from Bangkok night markets through Penang hawker stalls and arrive, fully formed, in a tin on your shelf.

We started in 2023 with one question on a notepad: why does every "premium" pantry sauce taste like it's been edited for committee? Three years, fourteen recipe drafts and a kitchen rebuild later, the answer is the five products on this page — bold, chef-led, and unwilling to apologise for either.

What you get is the instant upgrade to gourmet. Everyday cooking, dialled up. A pantry that quietly does the heavy lifting while you take the credit. The food still belongs to you. We're just here to make sure it doesn't taste like an apology.

№ 18 / The footprint

Glass & tin.
Forever recyclable.

Sustainability isn't a tagline at Sakaza — it's a packaging cost we chose to absorb. Here's what that means in numbers, and what's still on the to-do list.

Read the sustainability report (PDF) →

We chose glass for the sauces and tin for the noodles because both are infinitely recyclable without quality loss — unlike plastic, which downgrades with every cycle and eventually ends up in a landfill, a river, or a fish.

Every Sakaza bottle is rolled, not blown, from 38% post-consumer recycled glass. Every Sakaza tin is two-piece pressed steel, fully separable for kerbside collection. There is no plastic film, no laminated foil, no microplastic-lined cap. The only plastic in our supply chain is the seal under the bottle cap — and we're trialling a beeswax replacement for that, expected to ship in late 2026.

It costs us roughly 40% more per unit than the standard "premium" food-grade plastic our competitors use. It makes the product nearly 3× heavier to ship. We made the trade once, when we started, and we'll make it again every time we restock. Some things matter more than logistics — and the carbon math, when you factor in the recycle vs. landfill comparison over a 30-year horizon, eventually breaks in our favour.

We're not perfect. Our shipping cartons are recycled cardboard but the void fill is paper-pulp (better than air-pillow plastic, still energy-intensive). Our cold-chain logistics for fresh-batch shipments use refrigerated diesel trucks — we're piloting a switch to electric in Mumbai for 2026. Our website runs on a green-energy host. Our office is, mostly, run on solar.

Read the full footprint report for the granular breakdown — including the bits we're not proud of and the line items we're working to close before end of year.

№ 12 / Got questions

The fine print.

Everything we get asked about ingredients, shipping & storage. Anything we missed — give us a shout.

Are your sauces actually alcoholic?+
Nope — every Sakaza sauce is 0% spirit. We borrow the personality of iconic drinks like Tequila, Vodka & Gin, but the formulation is completely alcohol-free.
How do I cook the noodles?+
Boil water, drop the noodle nest in for three minutes, drain, toss with the seasoning. Add an egg or veggies and you've got a chef-style bowl in under five.
What do the sauces pair with?+
Spicy Tequila loves tacos & pizza. Fireball Vodka destroys on burgers and grilled meats. Honestly — open one and start dunking.
How long do they keep?+
Unopened: 12 months from production date. Once opened, refrigerate and enjoy within 30 days.
Do you ship pan-India?+
Yes. Free shipping on prepaid orders above ₹999. Same-day in Mumbai, 2–5 day everywhere else.
Are Sakaza products vegetarian?+
All our sauces and noodles are 100% vegetarian. Full allergen & ingredient information lives on every product page — including a granular sourcing breakdown of every spice.
Where do you source your spices?+
Single-origin, traceable to the farm. Tellicherry black pepper from a five-farm collective in Kerala; bird's-eye chillies from the Lim family farm in Penang; flower pepper from a Hanyuan cooperative in Sichuan. Read the full sourcing journal for the postcodes and the people.
What's in the packaging?+
Glass bottles (38% recycled content), two-piece pressed tin, recycled cardboard cartons, paper-pulp void fill. No plastic film, no foil laminate, no microplastic-lined caps. The only plastic in the supply chain is the bottle-cap seal, and we're trialling a beeswax replacement for Q4 2026.

Yes. Pick any product, choose "Subscribe & save 15%" at checkout, and we'll ship monthly, every two months, or quarterly — your call. Pause or cancel any time from your account.
